## Ha & Schmidhuber 2018 "World Models"

**Architectural Summary**

This foundational work decomposes world modeling into three components: (1) a VAE-based visual encoder (*V*) that compresses 64×64 images to a 32-dimensional latent vector, (2) a Memory module (*M*) implemented as a Mixture-of-Experts RNN (Mdn-RNN) that predicts future latent states given action sequences, and (3) a compact Controller (*C*) that operates in the latent space to solve tasks. The model learns on unlabeled video data, then freezes the encoder and trains the controller via evolution strategies. The key insight is that a simple policy can succeed in the learned latent space without ever seeing pixel-space.

**Training Signal & Loss Decomposition**

- VAE reconstruction loss: L_recon = ||obs - decode(z)||^2 + β * KL(q(z|obs) || p(z))
- Mdn-RNN next-state prediction: L_pred = mixture-of-Gaussians NLL for z_{t+1} given [z_t, a_t]
- Controller optimized via CMA-ES evolutionary strategy (fitness = reward), no gradients through the VAE/Mdn-RNN

**Prediction Horizon & Environments**

- 1-step predictions in latent space; multi-step rollouts for planning via learned dynamics
- Tested on CarRacing, Vizdoom, and Pendulum; strong results on CarRacing (learned a viable policy entirely from 10k random trajectories)
- No explicit uncertainty quantification (single-point predictions)

**Consequence Definition**

"Consequence" is next latent observation (z_{t+1}). The model does not explicitly model rewards or drives; reward comes only through the controller's evolution in latent space.

**Biological Pillar**

Predictive brain + embodiment. The compression into a low-dimensional latent space mirrors dimensionality reduction in biological sensory systems; the learned dynamics reflect the motor system's forward model.

**Reference Implementation**

Google Brain's official TensorFlow implementation (GitHub: hardmaru/world-models). https://github.com/hardmaru/world-models

**VRAM & Wall-Clock Estimate (64×64 proxy)**

- VRAM: ~2–3 GB
- Wall-clock: ~6–12 hours on RTX 4060 to train VAE + Mdn-RNN to convergence on 50k trajectories

## PlaNet (Hafner et al., 2019)

**Architectural Summary**

PlaNet introduces the Recurrent State-Space Model (RSSM) architecture, a cornerstone of modern world models. The RSSM is a hybrid latent-variable RNN with (1) deterministic hidden state *h_t* (RNN), (2) stochastic latent variable *z_t* ~ q(z_t | h_t, o_t), and (3) learned transition dynamics p(z_t | z_{t-1}, a_{t-1}, h_{t-1}). The decoder reconstructs observations; separate reward and value networks head off the latent state. Planning uses cross-entropy method (CEM) to optimize actions by sampling from the learned dynamics, making it a latent-space planning algorithm.

**Training Signal & Loss Decomposition**

- Reconstruction: L_recon = ||o_t - decode(z_t, h_t)||^2
- KL divergence: L_KL = KL(q(z_t | h_t, o_t) || p(z_t | z_{t-1}, a_{t-1}, h_{t-1}))
- Reward prediction: L_rew = (r_t - reward_net(z_t, h_t))^2
- Value prediction: L_val = (λ_t - value_net(z_t, h_t))^2

**Prediction Horizon & Environments**

- Effective planning horizon: 12–15 steps (CEM, 64 action samples, 10 iterations)
- Atari (limited success), DeepMind Control Suite (strong on locomotion), MuJoCo
- No explicit uncertainty in latent predictions (single distribution p(z_t))

**Biological Pillar**

Predictive brain + dreaming-replay. The RSSM's two-stream architecture (deterministic + stochastic) mirrors predictive coding in cortex; planning via sampling aligns with conceptual replay during sleep.

**Reference Implementation**

DeepMind PlaNet (TensorFlow 1.x). https://github.com/google-research/planet (Apache 2.0).

**VRAM & Wall-Clock Estimate**

- VRAM: ~3–4 GB
- Wall-clock: ~18–24 hours on RTX 4060

## Dreamer v1 (Hafner et al., 2020)

**Architectural Summary**

Dreamer v1 builds on PlaNet by adding policy learning in the latent space via gradient descent (rather than CEM planning). The actor and critic are trained purely on imagined trajectories within the learned world model — no real environment interaction in the inner loop.

**Training Signal & Loss Decomposition**

- World-model loss (same as PlaNet): L_wm = L_recon + β * L_KL + L_rew + L_val
- Policy loss (Actor-Critic): L_policy = -E_{z,h ~ imagined}[V(z, h)] + κ * entropy(π(a|z,h)) using straight-through gradient estimators
- Value loss: L_val = (λ_{imag} - V(z, h))^2

**Prediction Horizon & Environments**

- Planning horizon: 15–50 steps
- DeepMind Control Suite (27 tasks), some Atari; SOTA on continuous control at the time

**Biological Pillar**

Predictive brain + dreaming-replay + drives.

**Reference Implementation**

https://github.com/danijar/dreamer (Apache 2.0).

**VRAM & Wall-Clock Estimate**

- VRAM: ~4–5 GB
- Wall-clock: ~24–36 hours on RTX 4060

## Dreamer v2 (Hafner et al., 2021)

**Architectural Summary**

Dreamer v2 improves v1 by: (1) decoupling the prior p(z_t | z_{t-1}, a_{t-1}, h_{t-1}) and posterior q(z_t | h_t, o_t) more explicitly with KL balancing, (2) adding a two-loss-scale strategy (stop-gradient on the prior to prevent posterior collapse), (3) using a multi-step value target ("λ-return") for better credit assignment, and (4) scaling the architecture.

**Training Signal & Loss Decomposition**

- KL-balancing loss: L_KL = (1 - α) * KL(q || p) + α * KL(p || q) with α ∈ [0, 1] (typically 0.8–0.99)
- Stop-gradient RSSM update: Use sg(p_t) in the variational loss
- Actor loss with n-step returns
- RSSM: GRU hidden state (h) 200–600 dims, stochastic z 32–128 dims (Gaussian)

**Prediction Horizon & Environments**

- Planning horizon: 50–100 steps
- DeepMind Control Suite, Atari (Demon's Souls benchmark)

**Biological Pillar**

Predictive brain + dreaming-replay + drives + cognitive maps.

**Reference Implementation**

https://github.com/danijar/dreamer (Apache 2.0).

**VRAM & Wall-Clock Estimate**

- VRAM: ~5–6 GB
- Wall-clock: ~30–48 hours on RTX 4060

## Dreamer v3 (Hafner et al., 2023)

**Architectural Summary**

Dreamer v3 unifies discrete and continuous action spaces. Key changes: (1) replaces Gaussian latent z with categorical/discrete latent for better expressiveness, (2) gating in the transition model, (3) longer horizons (100–200 steps), (4) learnable scale for the KL term, (5) unifies discrete (Atari) and continuous (DMC) RL in a single recipe.

**Training Signal & Loss Decomposition**

- Categorical posterior: z_t ~ Cat(softmax(logits_q))
- Categorical prior: z_t ~ Cat(softmax(logits_p))
- KL: L_KL = KL(q(z_t) || p(z_t)) with automatic scale β_kl
- Actor-critic with λ-returns; entropy regularization
- RSSM: Gated transition; deterministic h; z typically 32 categoricals × 32 classes

**Prediction Horizon & Environments**

- Planning horizon: 100–200 steps
- Near-SOTA on Atari (57 games), DM Control (27 tasks)
- Categorical posterior allows marginalizing over stochastic latents for robust planning

**Biological Pillar**

Predictive brain + dreaming-replay + drives + cognitive maps + embodiment.

**Reference Implementation**

https://github.com/danijar/dreamer (v3 branch / dreamerv3 repo, Apache 2.0). Community PyTorch ports exist.

**VRAM & Wall-Clock Estimate**

- VRAM: ~6–8 GB (image envs); much less for symbolic-vector envs (~2-4 GB)
- Wall-clock: ~40–60 hours for image envs; faster for vector envs

## IRIS (Micheli et al., 2022)

**Architectural Summary**

IRIS replaces the RNN-based RSSM with a transformer-based world model. Observations and actions are tokenized (via a learned VQ-VAE codebook) into discrete tokens, then passed through a causal transformer to predict future tokens.

**Training Signal & Loss Decomposition**

- VQ-VAE tokenization loss (codebook + reconstruction + commitment)
- World-model transformer: cross-entropy on next-token prediction
- Policy transformer trained via RL on rollouts in tokenized world model

**Prediction Horizon & Environments**

- Limited by context window (512–2048 tokens), effective ~64–256 steps
- Atari-100k benchmark — reported strong sample efficiency

**Biological Pillar**

Predictive brain + cognitive maps.

**Reference Implementation**

(Verify URL during cite-check; the agent suggested an `evojax/iris` path that is likely wrong.)

**VRAM & Wall-Clock Estimate**

- VRAM: ~4–6 GB (transformer context window + VQ-VAE codebook)
- Wall-clock: ~20–30 hours on RTX 4060

## JEPA / V-JEPA (LeCun group, Assran et al., 2023)

**Architectural Summary**

JEPA predicts latent embeddings of future observations in a learned representation space rather than pixels. Encoder f → embeddings; predictor g forecasts future embeddings conditioned on actions; momentum-updated target encoder f_target. Loss: L = ||sg[f_target(o_{t+1})] - g(f(o_t), a_t)||^2.

**Training Signal & Loss Decomposition**

- L_jepa = ||stop_grad[f_target(o_{t+1})] - g(f(o_t), a_t)||_2^2
- f_target via EMA update
- No decoder, no pixel-level reconstruction

**Prediction Horizon & Environments**

- Single-step or few-step (2–4 steps); designed for representation, not long-horizon planning
- Atari, DM Control sample-efficiency benchmarks; real robot tasks

**Biological Pillar**

Predictive brain + embodiment. Stop-gradient target ≈ predictive coding's error signal.

**Reference Implementation**

https://github.com/facebookresearch/jepa (Apache 2.0). [Agent listed `vision_transformer` repo — wrong; use `jepa`.]

**VRAM & Wall-Clock Estimate**

- VRAM: ~2–3 GB
- Wall-clock: ~8–12 hours on RTX 4060

## MuZero (Schrittwieser et al., 2020)

**Architectural Summary**

MuZero learns a "model-free world model" that predicts value and policy directly without reconstructing observations. Encoder → abstract latent state s; learned dynamics function g predicts next states; prediction heads for value V(s) and policy π(s). MCTS planning in learned dynamics. No observation reconstruction.

**Training Signal & Loss Decomposition**

- Value loss: L_v = (v(s_t) - z_t)^2 (z_t = n-step MCTS-augmented return)
- Policy loss: L_π = -z_π_t * log(π(s_t))
- Dynamics implicitly trained via policy/value targets

**Prediction Horizon & Environments**

- Effective horizon set by MCTS depth (50–200 simulations/step)
- Atari (super-human), Go, Chess, Shogi

**Biological Pillar**

Drives + cognitive maps. Less grounded in predictive/sensory coding than world-model variants (no reconstruction).

**Reference Implementation**

DeepMind open-spiel reference (research-quality); community PyTorch impls (e.g., koulanurag/muzero).

**VRAM & Wall-Clock Estimate**

- VRAM: ~2–3 GB
- Wall-clock: ~12–18 hours on RTX 4060

## PredNet (Lotter et al., 2017) / Predictive Coding Networks

**Architectural Summary**

Hierarchical predictive coding as a deep recurrent architecture. Each layer l predicts the activity of the level below; prediction errors flow upward; ConvLSTM maintains representations.

**Training Signal & Loss Decomposition**

- Per-layer error: E^l = ||A^l - A^l_{pred}||^2
- Total loss: L_pred = Σ_l λ_l * E^l
- No actions in base PredNet; designed for video prediction

**Prediction Horizon & Environments**

- 5–10 frames of natural video (KITTI, UCF101)

**Biological Pillar**

Predictive brain (explicitly hierarchical). Direct instantiation of cortical predictive coding circuits.

**Reference Implementation**

https://github.com/coxlab/prednet (MIT).

**VRAM & Wall-Clock Estimate**

- VRAM: ~1.5–2.5 GB
- Wall-clock: ~4–8 hours on RTX 4060

## Intrinsic Curiosity Module — ICM (Pathak et al., 2017)

**Architectural Summary**

Intrinsic reward = forward-model prediction error in a learned feature space. Inverse model predicts actions from state transitions (anchors features to action-relevant info); forward model predicts next features.

**Training Signal & Loss Decomposition**

- Inverse model loss: L_inv (action prediction)
- Forward model loss: L_fwd = ||φ(s_{t+1}) - forward(φ(s_t), a_t)||^2
- Intrinsic reward: r_int = ½ ||φ(s_{t+1}) - forward(...)||^2
- Total: r_total = r_ext + β * r_int

**Biological Pillar**

Drives + predictive brain. Maps cleanly to SEEKING (curiosity / dopamine prediction-error).

**Reference Implementation**

OpenAI: https://github.com/openai/large-scale-curiosity (Apache 2.0).

**VRAM & Wall-Clock Estimate**

- VRAM: ~1–2 GB on top of base RL agent.

## Empowerment / Variational Empowerment (Mohamed & Rezende, 2015)

**Architectural Summary**

Maximize mutual information I(a_t; o_{t+k} | o_t) via a variational lower bound using a learned inverse model. Intrinsic reward = empowerment estimate.

**Biological Pillar**

Drives + embodiment. Aligns with active inference and agency theories.

**Reference Implementation**

No clean canonical repo; community reimplementations vary in quality.

## Random Network Distillation — RND (Burda et al., 2018)

**Architectural Summary**

A fixed random target network and a trained predictor; intrinsic reward = predictor error on the random network's output. Simple, hard to game, well-tuned for hard-exploration Atari games.

**Reference Implementation**

OpenAI: https://github.com/openai/random-network-distillation (MIT).

**VRAM & Wall-Clock Estimate**

- VRAM: ~0.8–1.5 GB on top of base RL.

## Homeostatic RL (Keramati & Gutkin, 2014, and follow-ons)

**Architectural Summary**

Augment MDP with internal-state (drive) vector h. Reward includes a homeostatic term r_homeo = -||h_t||^p. Policy conditions on (s, h). Actions reduce drives (eat → hunger ↓).

**Biological Pillar**

Drives + embodiment — directly maps allostatic regulation.

**Reference Implementation**

No canonical repo; theory-paper-driven, community impls sparse.

## Tolman-Eichenbaum Machine — TEM (Whittington et al., 2020)

**Architectural Summary**

HPC + PFC modules: HPC learns state representations and successor structure; PFC learns world model + policy. Multi-task objectives (observation, action, reward prediction).

**Biological Pillar**

Cognitive maps + predictive brain + drives. Most explicit hippocampal-prefrontal architectural mapping in current ML.

**Reference Implementation**

Author code available (verify URL during cite-check).

**VRAM & Wall-Clock Estimate**

- VRAM: ~2–3.5 GB
- Wall-clock: ~10–15 hours on RTX 4060

## Successor Representation / Successor Features (Barreto et al., 2016; Kulkarni)

**Architectural Summary**

Factor V(s) = φ(s)^T w. Learn ψ(s) = E[Σ_t γ^t φ(s_t)] via TD. Successor features extend to multi-task: Ψ_task(s) = φ(s)^T w_task. Aligns with successor-cell neural recordings (Stachenfeld et al. 2017).

**Biological Pillar**

Cognitive maps. Direct neural correlate.

**Reference Implementation**

Community impls; integrated into RLlib, TF-Agents.

**VRAM & Wall-Clock Estimate**

- VRAM: ~1.5–2.5 GB on top of base RL.

## Recommendation for v1 Trunk

### Primary: Dreamer V3 (small) with biology-aligned augmentations

**Rationale:**

1. **Most current, best-documented Dreamer family member.** v3 unifies discrete (gridworld actions) and continuous control under one recipe. Categorical latents are well-suited to symbolic/grid observations and avoid Gaussian-collapse failure modes that bite v1/v2.
2. **Fits 8 GB VRAM** at the small variant when trained on a vector-observation gridworld (~2–4 GB headroom). For RGB envs we'd be tighter at ~6–7 GB; still within budget.
3. **Biology grounding strong.** RSSM = predictive coding (deterministic + stochastic two-stream); imagination loop = dreaming-replay; reward+continuation+value heads → trivial to extend with Panksepp-style drive heads (SEEKING/FEAR/PLAY) and a successor-feature head (cognitive map).
4. **Reference impl mature.** danijar/dreamer is production-grade (JAX/TF). PyTorch ports exist but variable in quality — we'll evaluate during Phase 2.

**Concrete v1 recipe:**

- Symbolic-obs RSSM (no image encoder) with h ≈ 256, 16 categoricals × 16 classes for z (small).
- Replace the single reward head with **drive heads**: SEEKING (RND-style novelty), FEAR (predicted negative-outcome scalar), PLAY (information gain × inverse FEAR gating, per Panksepp's cross-system inhibition).
- Add a **successor-feature head** ψ(z, h) trained via TD.
- **Wake/sleep loop**: wake = standard Dreamer update on real rollouts; sleep = imagination rollouts with prioritized-by-prediction-error replay + multiplicative weight downscaling (Tononi & Cirelli SHY analogue, ~15% per cycle).
- Train on 100k–500k env steps.

### Fallback: Dreamer V2 if V3 reference impl proves unstable

V2 has clearer KL-balancing documentation and slightly lower VRAM. If the v3 PyTorch ports we find don't run cleanly on Windows + 8 GB, switch.

### Why not the others (briefly)

- **Ha & Schmidhuber 2018**: too simple — no policy learning gradient, no value/uncertainty.
- **PlaNet**: no policy learning; CEM planning is slow at inference.
- **IRIS**: transformer context-window cost; unclear it pays off at our scale.
- **JEPA/V-JEPA**: single-step predictor; not designed for long-horizon foresight.
- **MuZero**: no observation reconstruction → can't do biology probes that need to see what the model "imagines."
- **PredNet**: no actions.
- **Intrinsic motivation modules / Homeostatic RL / TEM / SR**: best as augmentations layered onto Dreamer, not as the trunk.
